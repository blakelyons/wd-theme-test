BRONZE Requirements (Grade 2nd - 5th) 
Participants must be able to swim the length of the pool (25 meters) in a time less than 1 minute.  Participants will be introduced to all four strokes as well as proper turns and starts.

SILVER Requirements (Grade 6th - 8th)
Participants should be able to swim a continuous 50 meters of each stroke, with their time to complete 50 meters of Freestyle under 1:15. Proper technique will be emphasized as well as endurance.

Gold Requirements (Grade 9th - 12th) 
Participants should be able to swim 10 x 100 Free on a 2:00 interval.  This class is for high school swimmers preparing for competition. 

Coaches reserve the right to move swimmers up and/or down to provide the best challenge for each swimmer's ability. 

Attendees must wear a mask upon entry and in common spaces, sanitize hands, practice physical distancing, and be prepared to have their temperature taken. Masks will not be required during the class.  Complete protocols will be emailed once registered. For a complete list of our health and safety protocols, visit our Resumption of Services page.

Lifeguard Class – Blended Learning

This class is a 29hr. course and participants must attend all days scheduled, for the session you register for.  The Blended Learning Lifeguard Class combines online learning with in person skills sessions. The fee includes all materials for the class.  Students who successfully complete this course will receive a certificate for Lifeguarding/First Aid/CPR/AED which is valid for 2 years.

  • Must be at least 15 years old on or before the final day of course. 
  • You will receive an email with the online session link 2 weeks prior to the class start date, and must complete the online portion prior to the first day of class.
  • You must bring your certificate of completion of the online portion with you to the first day of class or you will not be able to take the water portion of the training.  
  • All participants must successfully complete the following prerequisites the 1st day of class. Swim test will conducted at the beginning of the class, be prepared to swim. 

1. Swim 300 meters continuously, using these strokes in the following order:

     100 yards of front crawl using rhythmic breathing.

     100 yards of breaststroke using pull, breathe, kick and glide sequence.

     100 yards of either the front crawl or breaststroke

  1. Starting in the water, swim 20 yards using front crawl or breast stroke, surface dive 7-10  feet, retrieve a 10 lb object, return to the surface, swim 20 yards back to the starting point with the object and exit the water without using a ladder or steps, within 1 minute, 40 seconds.
  2. Tread water for 2 minutes without using your arms (using legs only).

What should I bring to Every Class?

  • Swimsuit & Towel, dry change of clothes
  • Sunscreen, sunglasses
  • Bring lunch or money for lunch for Saturday and/or Sunday class which is 9am-6pm.
  • A current e-mail, (The American Red Cross sends the certifications electronically to those participants who successfully pass the course).  It will take approximately 2 weeks after the last day of class for certifications to be sent.

Parent/Child Water Acclimation II

Session: 2021 Summer

Dates: July 11, 2021 - August 1, 2021

This class is for toddlers up to 3 years who have already completed a Parent/Child Water Acclimation class at the JCC.  It’s designed for children who are able to walk in/out of the water independently, fully submerge on their own, and understand and follow age-appropriate instructions from the teacher.   The parent or guardian must be in the water with the child.   All children must wear water diapers if they are not potty trained.  Classes are 30 minutes long, and will meet on Sundays.  A minimum of 6 participants maximum of 12.

Classes are subject to cancellation if there are not enough participants. Registration closes on the Thursday before the start of each session. 

Ages: 2 to 3 years
